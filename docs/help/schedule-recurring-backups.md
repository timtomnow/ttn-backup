ttn-backup can't run on its own in the background, so instead it creates a
calendar reminder. At the scheduled time your calendar nudges you, you tap the
link in the reminder, and the backup runs. This keeps backups regular without
relying on the app being open.

## Steps

1. Open the **Schedules** tab from the bottom bar.
2. Tap **+ New**.
3. Enter a **Name**, for example *Weekly Sunday backup*.
4. Choose a **Cadence** — **Daily**, **Weekly**, or **Monthly**.
5. Set the **Start date** and **Time** for the reminder.
6. Under **Apps to include**, tick the apps this schedule should back up.
7. Tap **Save**.
8. On the schedule's card, tap **.ics** to download the calendar file, then open
   it to add the reminder to your calendar.

## What you'll see

The schedule appears in the list with its cadence and the next run time. Once
you've imported the .ics, your calendar will remind you on schedule; tapping the
reminder's link runs the backup (one share-sheet tap to save on iPhone and
iPad).
